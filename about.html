About Us


GEOHORIZON


GeoHorizon is an annual national level technical symposium conducted by the Society of GeoInformatics Engineers. The motto of GeoHorizon is to share the knowledge of geospatial technologies and Remote Sensing among Engineering students. Initiated in 2005, it is one of the first Geospatial symposiums to be organized and held in India. "GeoHorizon" is one of the flagship events that aims to exhibit the talents of potential novices in the geospatial domain. It has a nationwide reach with industries and sponsorships that connects students of both undergraduate and graduate level. For the past twelve editions, GeoHorizon has been a platform for young enthusiasts to explore the depths of the latest in Geospatial technology and has also brought together and honoured the contributions of professional students in the field of Remote Sensing and GIS. It has broken myths, opened new avenues and redefined every perception of GeoInformatics.





SOCIETY OF GEOINFORMATICS ENGINEERS (SGE)


"The Society of GeoInformatics Engineers is a student council inaugurated in 2004 for improving the knowledge and skills of the students that are deemed imperative in the realm of Remote Sensing and GIS. SGE is intent on increasing the participation between the student and corporate community, and also enlightens the students as to what the corporate community expects. The society also conducts events and guest lectures by eminent professionals for the betterment of the students. It is the first student organization of its kind pursuing excellence in GeoInformatics community."

Our Theme - "IoT- The next GIS frontier"

We are excited to announce that the theme for GeoHorizon' 19 is "IoT- The next GIS frontier". Geography has been inextricably linked to modern technological processes and systems owing to the ever-evolving technology that has made data transfer and processing reach next level. We believe that augmentation of IoT data with interactive GIS mapping can significantly improve understanding of the data and may also lead to entirely new practices meant to make our world safer and more efficient, thus aiding in reaching a stage where actions could be caused through map manipulation in response to gathered data. Its pertinence without human-to-human or human-to-computer interaction to provide automatic reports, and the ability to assist businesses in performing a plethora of assessments and making relevant decisions through GIS is taken as the motif for this edition of GeoHorizon.
